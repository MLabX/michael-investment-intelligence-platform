# Investment Thesis Log

Long-term memory for MIIP's investment theses. This file records thesis framing,
assumptions, evidence needs, risks, and falsification triggers. It does not
record routine implementation details or individual placeholder scores.

## Platform-Level Thesis

MIIP exists to monitor structural investment themes and portfolio risk over
time, with enough discipline to distinguish signal, noise, narrative, and
position-level exposure.

The platform should help answer:

- What macro or liquidity regime are we in?
- Is AI capex accelerating, peaking, or becoming uneconomic?
- Is China being re-rated by investors, policymakers, or earnings revisions?
- Is robotics / physical AI moving from narrative to investable operating data?
- Is the portfolio overexposed to hidden factor, geography, liquidity, or theme
  concentration risk?

## Thesis: Macro Risk

### Working Hypothesis

Macro conditions can change the discount rate, liquidity backdrop, earnings
quality, and risk appetite enough to dominate company-level analysis.

### Evidence Needed

- Rates and yield-curve regime.
- Inflation trend and policy reaction function.
- Credit spreads and liquidity indicators.
- USD strength and global financial conditions.
- Equity market breadth and volatility.

### Key Risks

- Overfitting to backward-looking macro data.
- Treating one indicator as a regime call.
- Confusing macro narrative with portfolio-specific risk.

### Falsification / Review Triggers

- Macro signals disagree materially across rates, credit, liquidity, and breadth.
- Market action contradicts the assumed regime for multiple reporting periods.
- Portfolio drawdown occurs without being captured by the macro monitor.

## Thesis: AI Capex

### Working Hypothesis

AI infrastructure spending may remain a durable investment driver, but the
cycle's attractiveness depends on returns on invested capital, customer demand,
supply constraints, energy availability, and whether capex beneficiaries can
retain pricing power.

### Evidence Needed

- Hyperscaler capex guidance and revisions.
- Data center supply chain constraints.
- GPU / accelerator demand and inventory.
- Power, cooling, and grid bottlenecks.
- Revenue conversion from AI infrastructure to paid workloads.

### Key Risks

- Capex overbuild.
- Circular vendor financing or demand pull-forward.
- Margin compression as infrastructure supply catches up.
- Assuming all AI beneficiaries share the same economics.

### Falsification / Review Triggers

- Capex growth remains high while AI revenue conversion disappoints.
- Hyperscaler guidance turns cautious.
- Hardware supply constraints ease but margins fall faster than volumes rise.
- Energy or permitting constraints become the binding bottleneck.

## Thesis: China Re-rating

### Working Hypothesis

China-linked assets may re-rate if policy support, earnings revisions, capital
flows, and geopolitical risk premia improve together.

### Evidence Needed

- Policy signals and fiscal/monetary follow-through.
- Earnings revisions and margin trends.
- Foreign and domestic flow data.
- Currency stability and credit conditions.
- Geopolitical / sanctions risk indicators.

### Key Risks

- Policy announcements without durable transmission.
- Value traps caused by structurally impaired returns.
- Sudden geopolitical risk repricing.
- Index-level improvement hiding sector-level fragility.

### Falsification / Review Triggers

- Rally occurs without earnings revisions or flow confirmation.
- Policy stimulus fails to improve credit or consumption indicators.
- Currency stress offsets equity multiple expansion.

## Thesis: Robotics / Physical AI

### Working Hypothesis

Robotics and physical AI may become a major investment theme if model capability,
hardware cost curves, labor shortages, and real deployment economics converge.

### Evidence Needed

- Unit economics and deployment case studies.
- Sensor, actuator, battery, and compute cost trends.
- Manufacturing scale indicators.
- Customer adoption outside demos and pilots.
- Safety, liability, and regulatory constraints.

### Key Risks

- Demo-to-production gap.
- Hardware margins and service costs undermining software-like narratives.
- Long adoption cycles in industrial settings.
- Theme crowding before revenue quality appears.

### Falsification / Review Triggers

- Deployment announcements fail to convert into recurring revenue.
- Hardware reliability or safety incidents slow adoption.
- Gross margins stay structurally low despite scale.
- Public-market proxies diverge from actual robotics adoption.

## Thesis: Portfolio Risk

### Working Hypothesis

The portfolio can carry hidden concentration across factors, themes, geography,
currency, liquidity, and macro sensitivity even when individual positions appear
diversified.

### Evidence Needed

- Holdings, weights, and position history.
- Factor exposures and correlation clusters.
- Theme and supply-chain overlap.
- Liquidity and drawdown behavior.
- Scenario sensitivity to rates, USD, China, AI capex, and volatility shocks.

### Key Risks

- False precision from incomplete holdings data.
- Correlation breakdown in stress periods.
- Ignoring tax, liquidity, and personal constraints.
- Crossing from decision support into personalized advice.

### Falsification / Review Triggers

- Portfolio losses cluster in supposedly unrelated positions.
- Risk monitor fails to identify a major shared exposure.
- Report language starts implying recommended trades or allocations.
